Core Philosophy

AI as a Learning Partner, Not a Replacement

The goal is to enhance your understanding, not bypass the learning process.

Think of AI as:

  • A tutor who guides you
  • A pair programming partner
  • A reference book you can ask questions
  • A code reviewer who provides feedback

NOT as:

  • Someone to do your homework
  • A shortcut to avoid learning
  • A replacement for your own thinking

The 80/20 Rule

80%

You should write at least 80% of your code yourself

AI should assist with the remaining 20%:

  • When you're genuinely stuck after trying
  • Understanding complex concepts
  • Reviewing and improving your work
  • Learning new patterns or techniques
  • Debugging stubborn issues

Warning: If you rely on AI for more than 20% of your code, you're likely not learning effectively!

‚úó Inappropriate Uses of AI

Never Do This:

1. Direct Solution Generation

  • "Write the complete code for Lab 3"
  • "Give me the answer to assignment question 5"
  • Copying entire solutions without understanding

2. Bypassing the Learning Process

  • Using AI before attempting the problem yourself
  • Not reading the lab materials or documentation
  • Relying on AI instead of debugging yourself

3. Submitting AI Code as Your Own

  • Copy-pasting without modification or understanding
  • Not being able to explain your submitted code
  • Using AI-generated comments without verification

Best Practices for AI Usage

1. Always Attempt First

1Read lab instructions carefully
2Review relevant documentation
3Try to solve the problem yourself
4Write pseudocode or outline approach
5Only then, ask AI for specific guidance

Key Point: Struggling is part of learning. Don't rush to AI at the first sign of difficulty!

Understand Before Implementing

When AI provides code, you must:

1Read through it line by line
2Look up any functions you don't recognize
3Modify it to fit your specific needs
4Add comments in your own words
5Test it to ensure you understand how it works

Never copy-paste AI code without understanding it!

If you can't explain the code, you don't understand it well enough to submit it.

Suggested Workflow for Labs

1Read and Plan (No AI) - Understand requirements, plan your approach
2Initial Implementation (Minimal AI) - Write basic structure, implement what you know
3Learning Phase (Strategic AI) - Ask about concepts you're struggling with
4Implementation (Your Code) - Write based on understanding, AI for debugging only
5Review and Improve (AI as Reviewer) - Get feedback, implement improvements yourself
6Testing (Minimal AI) - Test thoroughly, debug independently when possible

‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags: Signs of Misuse

Warning Signs You're Misusing AI:

  • You can't explain how your code works
  • You're spending more time prompting AI than coding
  • You didn't attempt the problem before asking AI
  • You're copying large blocks of code without modification
  • You're asking AI to complete entire assignments
  • You feel anxious about coding without AI available

If you recognize these signs, it's time to step back and refocus on learning!

Assessment & Academic Integrity

What Will Be Evaluated:

  • Your understanding of the code, not just if it works
  • Your ability to explain and defend implementation choices
  • Your problem-solving process, not just the final solution
  • Whether you can modify and extend your code

You May Be Asked To:

  • Explain your code line by line
  • Modify your implementation on the spot
  • Debug issues without AI assistance
  • Discuss alternatives and trade-offs

Academic Honesty: Submitting code you don't understand is still academic dishonesty, even if AI is permitted.
